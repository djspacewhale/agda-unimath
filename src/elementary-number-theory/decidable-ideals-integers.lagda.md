## Idea

The decidable ideals in the ring of integers (those whose underlying subsets are
decidable) are precisely those generated by some `n : ℤ`. Without loss of
generality, we may even say they're the ideals generated by a unique `n : ℕ`.

## Definitions

### The ideals generated by a given `n : ℤ`

These are also called the **principal ideals** of `ℤ`.

```agda
principal-ideal-ℤ : (n : ℤ) → ideal-Ring lzero ℤ-Ring
principal-ideal-ℤ n = ideal-subset-Ring ℤ-Ring λ m → Id-Prop ℤ-Set m n
```

These may also be given by `n : ℕ`.

```agda
principal-ideal-ℤ' : (n : ℕ) → ideal-Ring lzero ℤ-Ring
principal-ideal-ℤ' n = ideal-subset-Ring ℤ-Ring λ m → Id-Prop ℤ-Set m (int-ℕ n)
```
